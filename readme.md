## Stone Setting Microscopes

- [BenchCam 4K 75FPS](https://www.engraver.com/benchcam-4k-75fps-microscope-camera-for-jewelers-stone-setters-and-engravers/) - Purpose-built 4K 75fps microscope camera with HDMI, USB, WiFi, and on-screen measurement; turns stereo scopes into recording and demo stations.
- [Leica A60](https://www.leica-microsystems.com) - Stereo microscope widely adopted for stone setting and bench inspection.
- [Leica S9i](https://www.microscopecentral.com/products/leica-s9i-gemological-jewelers-microscope-9-8x-88x-magnification) - Stereo microscope with FusionOptics depth of field and built-in 10MP camera for live view, documentation, and client demos.
- [Meiji Techno](https://www.meijitechno.com) - More affordable stereo microscopes used in setting and gemology.

## Automated Stone Setting

- [SuperbMelt Automatic Stone Setting Machine](https://www.superbmelt.com) - Vision-guided robotic system that glues and sets stones at up to 7 diamonds per second; used for production pavé and channel work.
- [Polyintech Wax Setting Robot](https://www.polyintech.com) - Multi-nozzle system that places stones into wax models before casting, enabling cast-in-place workflows at thousands of stones per hour.
- [Hajet Wax Setting Robot](https://www.hajettool.com) - High-throughput wax stone setting system with auto stone feeding and heating.
- [ALVMAC](https://www.alvmac.com) - High-precision gantry-style pick-and-place systems for watch and jewelry assembly and inspection.

## Engraving

- [GRS GraverSmith](https://www.grstools.com) - Workshop-standard pneumatic graver console used for both engraving and production stone setting.
- [GRS GraverMach AT](https://www.grstools.com) - Higher-end GRS power unit favored by full-time engravers.
- [Lindsay AirGraver](https://www.lindsayengraving.com) - Pneumatic graver, a reference for hand engraving.
- [PepeTools IronBrush](https://pepetools.com/products/ironbrush%E2%84%A2-pneumatic-engraving-tool) - Pneumatic engraver and stone-setting tool released in 2025, expanding PepeTools' bench equipment line.
- [Andu Engraving](https://anduengraving.com) - Lower-cost pneumatic engravers; a common entry option for jewelers getting into hand engraving without the GRS or Lindsay investment.
- [Gravograph](https://www.gravograph.com) - Computerized engraving and marking systems used in retail jewelry.

## Bench Tools

- [Arbe Bench Vacuum System](https://www.arbemachine.com/view/jewelers-bench-vacuum-system/) - Under-bench dual-filtration vacuum with metal-dust bag and charcoal solder-fume filter; popular integrated extraction for clean bench work.
- [Durston Rolling Mill](https://www.durston.com/collections/rolling-mills) - UK-built rolling mills that are the industry reference for sheet and wire reduction at the bench.
- [Foredom](https://www.foredom.net) - Flexshaft rotary tools that are the workhorse of nearly every bench.
- [GRS BenchMate](https://www.grstools.com) - Modular ball-vise platform around which most modern setting and engraving workflows are built.
- [Knew Concepts](https://www.knewconcepts.com) - Precision titanium and aluminum jeweler's saws.
- [NSK EMax EVOlution](https://www.nskamericacorp.com/series/emax-evolution/) - Brushless micromotor system with digital speed control to 40,000 RPM, swappable handpieces, and low vibration; widely used as an alternative to flexshafts.
- [Quatro Dust Collectors](https://www.quatroair.com/) - HEPA filtration systems engineered for jewelers, mounted under benches to capture gold, silver, and polishing dust.
- [Saeshin Strong](https://www.saeshin.us) - High-power brushless micromotors with strong torque and value pricing; widely adopted Korean brand.
- [Smith Little Torch](https://www.contenti.com/shop-by-brand/smith-torches) - Precision multi-flame oxygen-fuel soldering torch standard for bench work; sold via Contenti and other distributors.

## Gemology & Identification

- [De Beers DiamondView](https://www.debeersgroup.com) - Reference instrument for confirming natural vs. synthetic origin.
- [GIA iD100](https://www.gia.edu) - GIA's reference HPHT/CVD synthetic-diamond screener.
- [GL Gem Spectrometer](https://www.cigem.ca/research-technology/gl-gem-spectrometer) - Portable UV-Vis-NIR with Point & Shoot software and a 200+ gem/diamond/PL spectral library; upgradeable, popular for field and lab gem ID.
- [Magilabs EXA](https://www.gemmoraman.com/products/exa/) - Portable photoluminescence probe that screens loose or mounted natural diamonds in under 1 second; refers all synthetics and simulants.
- [Magilabs GemmoFtir](https://www.gemmoraman.com/products/gemmoftir/) - Affordable FTIR spectrometer with built-in gem-specific analysis for diamond, emerald, sapphire, and turquoise.
- [Magilabs GemmoRaman](https://www.gemmoraman.com) - Affordable Raman spectrometer for advanced gem testing.
- [Magilabs GemmoSphere](https://www.gemmoraman.com/products/gemmosphere/) - Compact UV-Vis-NIR spectrometer for diamond origin testing and colored stone absorption spectra, loose or mounted.
- [OGI Diamond Scanner](https://www.ogisystems.com) - Diamond scanning and 4Cs grading hardware.
- [Presidium](https://www.presidium.com.sg) - Handheld gem testers, refractometers, and color graders.
- [Sarine Galaxy](https://www.sarine.com) - Inclusion mapping and AI-assisted diamond grading hardware.
- [Yehuda Sherlock](https://www.yehuda.com) - Synthetic-diamond detection devices used in retail.
- [Bruker XRF Analyzers](https://www.bruker.com/en/products-and-solutions/elemental-analyzers/handheld-xrf-spectrometers.html) - X-ray fluorescence spectrometers used in gem labs for trace-element analysis, treatment identification, and origin work.

## Synthetic Gemstone Production

- [Verneuil Flame Fusion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Verneuil_process) - Powder melted in oxy-hydrogen flame and crystallized on a seed rod; the fastest, cheapest method, dominant for synthetic ruby and sapphire.
- [Czochralski Pulling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Czochralski_method) - Crystal slowly pulled from a molten crucible with computer-controlled rotation; produces high-quality large crystals.
- [Hydrothermal Growth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrothermal_synthesis) - High-pressure autoclaves mimicking natural formation; favored for synthetic emerald and quartz.
- [Chatham](https://www.chatham.com) - Long-running flux-grown emerald, ruby, and sapphire producer; founded in 1938.
- [Element Six (De Beers)](https://www.e6.com) - Industrial-scale CVD lab-grown diamond producer; pioneered the modern process.
- [Diamond Foundry](https://www.diamondfoundry.com) - Vertically integrated CVD lab-grown diamond company.
- [WD Advanced Materials](https://www.wdadvancedmaterials.com) - US-based CVD diamond producer (formerly WD Lab Grown Diamonds; rebranded 2025).

## Gemstone Treatments

- [HPHT Treatment](https://4cs.gia.edu/en-us/diamond-treatments/) - Extreme pressure and heat in industrial presses used to improve diamond color and clarity.
- [Gemstone Irradiation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gemstone_irradiation) - Linear accelerators or gamma sources used to create or enhance color in topaz, diamond, and other stones.
- [Beryllium Lattice Diffusion](https://www.gia.edu/gems-gemology/summer-2003-beryllium-diffusion-ruby-sapphire-emmett) - High-temperature element diffusion that produces vivid sapphire color enhancement.
- [Zachery Treatment for Turquoise](https://www.gia.edu/gems-gemology/spring-1999-identification-zachery-treated-turquoise-fritsch) - Vacuum and pressure polymer impregnation that stabilizes turquoise without significantly altering its appearance.
- [Cactus Juice for Turquoise (Turntex)](https://www.turntex.com) - Epoxy stabilizer widely used to harden turquoise and other porous stones.
- [Yehuda Fracture Filling](https://www.yehuda.com) - Diamond fracture-filling treatment using glass infusion under pressure; the brand that originated the process.
- [Diamond Laser Drilling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diamond_clarity) - Micro-laser channels used to remove inclusions, often followed by fracture filling.
- [Heat Treatment](https://www.gia.edu/gia-faq-heat-treatment-affect-sapphire-value) - Precision computer-controlled kilns used for color improvement in sapphire, tanzanite, and other stones.
